Business brief

Bill splitting app without in-app payments

This brief explains what the app does, what users experience, what the business team needs to validate, and what payment research should cover before the product considers any payment integration.

View App Journey Payment Research Business Actions
Current strategy

Split first. Pay outside the app.

The MVP calculates what each person owes and tracks settlement status. Users complete repayment through their preferred external method.

No money movement No wallet, no card processing, no payouts, no stored bank details.
Product overview

What is going on in the app?

One user pays a bill first, uses the app to split the receipt, then tracks whether friends have paid them back outside the app.

Core problem

Group bills are annoying when people ordered different items, shared some dishes, and need tax or service charge split fairly.

Core solution

The app captures the receipt, turns it into selectable items, lets participants choose what they bought, calculates exact shares, and shows who owes the payer.

Core boundary

The app does not transfer money. It only displays repayment instructions and lets users mark settlements manually.

User journey

The app flow in six steps

This is the business-level journey the team should understand before discussing monetization, partnerships, or payment integrations.

1

Take photo

User takes or uploads a receipt photo to start the split.

2

Review receipt

App reads items, totals, tax, and service charge for correction.

3

Invite people

User shares a link or QR so friends can join the bill.

4

Select items

Participants choose the items they bought or shared.

5

Review split

Payer checks claims, shared items, tax, discounts, and final amounts.

6

Track settlement

User shares the summary and marks people as paid after external repayment.

Competitor research

Receipt-splitting app patterns to study

Split Mobile is the direct reference app to study. Its public site describes a receipt-first flow where the host uploads a receipt, shares a secure link, guests claim their items or split evenly, and settlement happens through card or supported checkout.

App / reference Observed pattern What we should learn
Split Mobile Host uploads or snaps a receipt, shares a guest link, guests claim items or choose even split, then settle through checkout. Study the receipt-first flow, guest link experience, item-claiming UX, and payment scope we are intentionally excluding from MVP.
Splyt Users scan a receipt, share a link or QR, and friends tap what they ordered. Study link/QR joining, no-download participant flow, and item claiming UX.
SplitEven Receipt scan extracts items, then users assign or split items and share a browser link. Study how much detail recipients need before trusting the total.
splitty Starts from snapping a receipt and tweaking who got what, including shared dishes. Study shared-item controls and how defaults reduce effort.
ReceiptSplit Only the organizer needs the app; friends receive a text breakdown. Study whether we can avoid forcing participant sign-up in MVP.
Payment research

What the business team should research

The payment workstream is research-only for MVP. The goal is to understand user behavior, market expectations, compliance burden, and future partnership options.

Recommendation: keep payment out of the MVP. Research payment behavior now, then decide later whether to add payment links, payment QR display, or real provider integration.

Research workstream A: User payment behavior

  • Which external methods do users already use: cash, bank transfer, DuitNow, Touch 'n Go, GrabPay, PayPal, Venmo, Zelle, or others?
  • Do users want payment instructions shown inside the bill summary?
  • Do users prefer QR images, phone numbers, usernames, or plain text instructions?
  • How often do users forget to pay after seeing a split?
  • Would reminders feel helpful or annoying?

Research workstream B: Market and competitor analysis

  • Compare Splitwise, Tab, Settle Up, Tricount, Venmo groups, and local wallet workflows.
  • Identify which competitors support receipt itemization, settlement status, reminders, and payment links.
  • Document pricing, free limits, and premium features.
  • Check user complaints about payment friction, receipt scanning, and group tracking.
  • Find gaps that can become our positioning.

Research workstream C: Provider feasibility

  • Analyze Split Mobile's payment model: guests settle by card or supported checkout, while reimbursement stays tied to the receipt.
  • List payment providers by region: Stripe, PayPal, Billplz, Razer Merchant Services, PayNet/DuitNow, wallet providers.
  • Check whether each supports payment links, QR, webhooks, refunds, and settlement reporting.
  • Check onboarding requirements, business account requirements, approval timelines, fees, and supported countries.
  • Confirm whether peer-to-peer repayment is allowed by provider terms.
  • Identify sandbox access requirements and technical documentation quality.

Research workstream D: Risk and compliance

  • Clarify what changes legally if the app initiates or receives payments.
  • Understand KYC, AML, chargebacks, disputes, fraud, and data retention obligations.
  • Check whether the company can avoid holding funds by using payment links only.
  • Ask legal/accounting how revenue, fees, refunds, and taxes would be handled.
  • Define a clear "we do not process payments" user disclaimer for MVP.
Future payment options

Possible directions after MVP

These are not MVP requirements. They are decision paths the business team can evaluate after user validation.

Option What it means Business complexity Best use case
Manual instructions Show text like "Pay Alex by DuitNow to 012-3456789". Low MVP and early market validation.
QR image display User uploads their external payment QR image for others to scan. Low to medium Markets where QR repayment is common.
Payment links Generate links through a provider, but provider handles checkout. Medium When users want smoother repayment but the app should avoid holding funds. This is the closest future path to Split Mobile's checkout-style settlement.
Marketplace payments Use providers like Stripe Connect to route funds to users. High Later stage product with enough volume to justify compliance work.
Bank or wallet integration Integrate local rails such as DuitNow through approved providers. High Country-specific expansion with strong partner support.
Business actions

What the business team should do next

These tasks help the team reduce uncertainty before engineering invests in payment-related features.

1. Interview users

Talk to friends, students, roommates, travelers, and office teams about how they split bills today.

2. Test prototype

Use the key screens to test if users understand selecting their own items, owed amounts, and settlement tracking.

3. Map payment habits

Identify the top external repayment methods by region and user segment.

4. Contact providers

Ask providers about payment links, QR support, fees, eligibility, and whether peer repayment is allowed.

5. Check compliance

Confirm with legal/accounting what features trigger financial compliance obligations.

6. Decide later

Only add payment integration after users prove that payment friction is a major blocker.

Research questions

Questions to answer before payment integration

  • What percentage of users would be satisfied with manual settlement tracking?
  • Which repayment methods are most common in our launch market?
  • Would users trust the app more or less if it tried to process payments?
  • Are users willing to pay fees for smoother repayment?
  • Can payment links solve enough of the problem without full money movement?
  • What provider terms restrict peer-to-peer repayment or personal use?
  • What is the minimum transaction volume needed to justify integration cost?
